Hire Me Direct

Find the right person for the job

Client

Hire Me Direct

Role

User research, strategy, IxD

Purpose

UX optimization of contractor portal

Status

Prototyped and pitched in March 2016



The Brief

Hire Me Direct came to us for help in improving a pre-existing digital experience where contractors providing A/V services could connect with A/V freelancers that would help in carrying out large event contracts.

Our assignment was to optimize the buyer portal experience, whereby a contractor could search, hire, manage and pay a freelancer should they fit the criteria for the job.


Our Approach

Our approach was to start exploring the analog domains of event planning and A/V services. In understanding how key players in the domain operate, we would then be able to pick the appropriate models that solve these analog pain points.

Understand

Our primary goal was to understand the event planning and A/V domains along with the behaviors of its key players. Given the niche area we were exploring, finding users that fit the target profile was difficult.

Interviews and Affinity Mapping

We asked our client for SME contacts, whom we consulted for their knowledge on the target user, and their ability to provide contacts for initial user interviews. After gaining a better idea of the target user profile, we were able to source more interviewees from our personal networks. We took the insights from our interviews to create a task model of the contractor's journey. Furthermore, we performed a market analysis of the A/V freelancing space to get a better idea of Hire Me Direct’s competitors. We used the combined insights from our research to create target user groups.

The Experienced Contractor

Goals

  • Provide exceptional service to end clients
  • Hire trusted specialists well in advance
  • Streamline crew scheduling and payment process

Frustrations

  • Outdated scheduling and document management tools
  • Inefficient communication management
  • Laborious process for finding skilled technicians

The Inexperienced Contractor

Goals

  • Build a reputation for good work in the industry
  • Find the right technicians in a short amount of time
  • Streamline scheduling and payment process

Frustrations

  • Small network of trusted contacts
  • Assessing risk of finding a tech outside of immediate network
  • Managing payments and legal overhead

Key Insights

  • This is a community of connected people who prefer to work within their networks. (referrals are everything)
  • Open communication on payments, scheduling and responsibilities is crucial to reach clients expectations.
  • There are limited tools for communication and scheduling
  • Current users of the service do not use it for it’s intended purpose of “finding the right guy” but rather find it a helpful resource in processing payments as a means to avoid the hassle of dealing with legal paperwork
  • A freelancer's location weighs heavily when looking to hire on short notice

Define

The insights from our research exploration allowed us to accurately define who our users were, how they behaved, and what their frustrations were. We noticed a particular theme in our investors’ frustrations and identified that as the overarching problem that needed to solved.

The Problem

A/V service contractors lack adequate tools to find skilled sub-contractors and manage the projects they put them on.

Shaping the solution with product values

As a first step in shaping our solution, we established a core set of product values. Product values ensure that any decision made in regards to the product (from features, content structure and copy to layout, color and typeface) align themselves to the core needs of the target user and the overarching problem trying to be solved.

The product values we created in this specific instance sprouted from our insights on the needs and frustrations of the inexperienced contractor and sub-contractor.


Growth

Hire Me Direct need to scale to a larger audience and acquire 10 more technicians in 10 major cities. This platform could start that conversation and grow to a scale beyond those limitations.


Accessibility

Anybody from various skill levels should be able to use the site, work among their circles, attain new skills and grow their own freelance business.


Opportunity

Low level technicians now have the opportunity to work on a platform that caters to their industry and grow their own circle of contacts.


Trust

Clients won't use this service unless they can advocate it’s value by establishing; trust transparency and open communication with their technicians.


Selectiveness

The selective vetting process reinforces the ability to get the best professional technicians in the field and ensure a quality product.

Strategize

Based off our definition of the problem and product values, we were able to focus on a core feature set to build the solution around. We whiteboarded our content strategy and soon after started exploring models of best-in-class UI patterns for our interactions.

Core feature set

  • Enhance the scheduling tools
  • Build communication channels to ensure good management
  • Streamline payment for quick and easy financial transactions
  • Establish a network of easily discoverable freelancers with strong reputations

Prototype

Given our insights on effective models from our best in class analysis, our next step was to incorporate these models into prototypes built towards user-testing.

Given the complexity of the system, mocking up all the screens in a program like Sketch or Axure would have eaten up a lot our time. Instead, we decided to sketch the screens our and prototype them in InVision. The sketching allowed for quick iteration and revision as needed.

Iterate

After creating interactive prototypes, our next goal was to assess whether our proposed solution was valid in the hands of a real user. We decided on a set of strict criteria, but given the niche domain and time constraint we faced of 48 hours, it was difficult to find user that find the criteria of acceptable user testers. To get around this, we ended up widening our search pool by scaling down the criteria.

Key Insights

  • The Air Bnb model for searching for freelancers tested well
  • Users liked the BaseCamp interface for project management, however, found the scheduling ticket-style interface confusing
  • Users liked seeing a tiered network like LinkedIn’s community model
  • Usability issues with payment

Revisions

We now had to take the key features from previous testing and craft an improved experience. At this point, we were still unsure as to whether to continue with a web or mobile. If we chose to go either way, how would do we elegantly fit in features concepted for the other platform?

Delivery

After testing and iterating on our prototypes, we converged on a single solution that we delivered to HMD. Our final deliverables consisted of Hi-Fi annotated wireframes along with suggestions on how to move forward.

Moving Forward


Adopting the Venmo model for creating payment-based network

Contractors currently on the site use it to avoid legal overhead in processing payments. Furthermore, they require their subcontractors to create an account on the site in order to pay them. These same contractors will only hire people that they have either previously worked with, or someone that has been vouched for by a party they trust; assuming that party has worked with said subcontractor in the past.

Given that a financial transaction between these parties can be used as proof of work, much like Venmo, we can leverage the financial transactions occurring on the site as proof of two parties having interacted with each other, and start building a tiered network of professionals based on whom they have worked with in the past. This makes professionals easily discoverable while also settling the ever-prevalent trust issues around working with a subcontractor whom a hiring contractor has not yet had direct professional contact with.


Adopting an Uber model for location-based discoverability of service technicians

Situations of needing to hire a technician the day of an event are par for the course in this industry. In these situations, proximity to the venue plays a large part in the time and travel cost it takes for a subcontractor hired the day of an event to reach the venue and perform the task they were hired for. Very few subcontractors take a job where the travel costs exceed the compensation they receive for the job.

This can easily be solved by leveraging the Uber model for location-based service discoverability, where a client enters their criteria and the system finds the nearest service provider that accepts the request.

Final Thoughts

In wrapping up this project, we learned a lot about working within the technical constraints of a pre-existing platform, development constraints of what was technically feasible, and the value in being able to communicate design concepts to a client. In speaking to the final constraint, gained a better understanding in how to step outside the boilerplate UX process everyone learns, and crafting deliverables that bypass the ambiguity many clients experience on the receiving end of being presented with such deliverables.

What is a deliverable?

The purpose of a deliverable is to enhance the quality of knowledge transfer between two parties. In that respect, the presentation of that knowledge can take many forms, and there is no “correct” way to create a journey map, persona, mental model, etc. Ultimately, the inclusion or exclusion of the information presented (along with the visual representation of that information) is driven by who the information is being communicated to and what they value.


The lessons I learned in this project would further influence my approach to solving problems in my next project for GGP Malls.
